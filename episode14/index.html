The families on Rumueri Hill must be descendants, somehow, of the Netabare lineage. Not that I can... exactly put it together. It's all still a little messy. But that's the only way it all fits.

"Seems like you're thinking... dumb things. Sorry. No simple way to say it. Don't get carried away. My bad, I told you to keep thinking. But some thoughts... not a great track. Both of us have good hearing. Kin of spirit, not blood." Clarie speaks a little haltingly.

"O-okay..."

"Please don't... tell my mom I said that." I say it pretty quietly, feeling stupid all the while.

It's not like I was trying to be mean. I was just thinking. It's not like any of this matters, anyway. They're just NPCs... But... Maybe they can feel hurt or something. I just-

Shiori steps in close, her face popping up close to mine.

"It's okay, Meru." She gives me a comforting smile like she always this, even when she doesn't quite get it. "I know you're just trying your best."

"Y-yeah..." I shuffle a bit and look up, and there's Clarie looking guilty and shuffling herself like she just kicked a puppy.

Maybe we really are kin of spirit.

"I should go." She finally says it after a long awkward silence. "...wasn't trying to be mean. You have a good friend, though. Okay, bye."

It almost feels like she tries to get a running start the way she flips around and starts speed walking. But I call out to stop her.

"Wait! Clarie... Are you just going to leave us to our devices here?" I ask, a little confused more than anything.

Her back's still turned to us. "What harm could kids do? Just make sure not to get kidnapped. That's all. Watch out for Hannah if she comes at you with a rag."

...Is that supposed to be a joke? That is an extremely uncomfortable joke to be telling children, and Shiori just nods at the advice as if she got told not to accept candy from strangers.

But before she's off to the races once more, she offers some last parting words. "Meru. Soon... if you hear things. Even newer things. You can ask me. Okay? Don't keep it in."

Aaaaand she's gone, up the spiral staircase we came in through, speeding past the clerestory. She must have felt subconscious with us still watching her because she starts ducking a bit as if it means anything.

"Wow, what a weirdo," Alexa snorts. She looks as smug as ever. "No squealing this time?"

Shiori did squeak a little, actually.

And her rapid appearance does shock me, but it was almost too rapid this time. I didn't have time to yelp or scream, and I just turn my gaze toward her coolly. She's usually a little annoying, but she must have really come to piss me off today.

She really does go for the sore spots.

"That's what I said, yes."

No, seriously, I am this close to hitting her.

But I keep it in, and Alexa leads us to the far side of the room, where there's something like a bar tucked in the corner. There's tons of bottles of expensive looking alcohol around. Many have in fact been opened, but somehow I get the sense it's been years since anyone drank any of this.

"People are always building Speak Easies they'll never actually use. It's a shame, really." Alexa picks up what looks like a bottle of bourbon to read the labels. She's got the appearance of an older teen so it's almost more inappropiate of a look than if Shiori and I played with it. Erm, I mean, picked it up.

Alexa walks over to a register tucked away in a small compartment behind the bar counter. Click. She presses a button to pop it open, and there's the familiar sound of paper moving.

"This one sounds kinda... gross." I say. Maybe it's my sensitive hearing, but the way it's moving around under all these receipts reminds me of a worm.

And sure enough a snitch comes out of The Codex to cluck and pluck, pecking and the papers till it grabs one which wriggles disgustingly in its clutches.

It's an... order sheet? Filled orders for diethyl ether. I don't know what that is, but it sounds pretty heavy.

I've got no comeback. All I can do is anxiously mumble in response, and Shiori takes on that withdrawn and empty look again.

I know it's the least important thing right now. But can't she be just... nicer?

I glance up, and there's Alexa staring me straight in the eye. Again, I can't help but notice they're beautiful and somehow pale. Almost... iridescent.

"The past was horrible. But the future is more important. This isn't a Haunted House. You're not just here on a ride. What you do from now on does matter. Especially now that the murders are about to start." Alexa takes a deep breath and lets out an even deeper sigh. "Don't let the past drag you into a pit."

There's light in those eyes again, and she's brimming with something she clearly hasn't had in a while. Hope.

And the gut feeling I have that what she's hoping for won't happen fills a tiny, hidden part of me with quiet despair.

"...I won't promise anything like that. I'm just telling you to keep your eyes open. Keep looking at what's around you, and don't stop observing."

And if you don't... it's gonna be bad. I'm not gonna spoonfed you any more, because it's not gonna help you one bit if you can't think for yourself."

It's annoying because, well, I figured it was something like that. But having confirmation is completely different than even the most educated speculation.

"Okay. We can do it. If that's what it takes." Even Yorick's got a determined look, his little eyes squinting in fierce, slime determination.

"...Good. I'll leave you to it then. And read that snitch already, damn." Alexa says.
